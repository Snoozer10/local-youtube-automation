## 3. MASTER EXPRESSIVE TAG LEXICON
You must strategically inject these specific acting and pacing modifiers immediately before target words or sentences to force expressive variety at stable temperatures. Do not use any physical breath or sighing tags.

### A. Tone & Mood Modifiers
* `[tone: conversational]` - Relaxed, natural baseline storytelling.
* `[tone: smirk]` - Slightly higher pitch, vocalizing a subtle smile or witty remark.
* `[tone: confidential]` - Dropping the volume and register, leaning in for a secret or harsh truth.
* `[tone: skeptical]` - An incredulous, slightly questioning tone; excellent for dry sarcasms.
* `[tone: serious]` - Authoritative, earnest, and direct; used to emphasize a critical point.
* `[tone: excited]` - Slightly higher pitch and energy; used for engaging hooks or fun facts.
* `[tone: deadpan]` - Completely flat, dry, and sarcastic.

### B. Inflection & Pitch Controls
* `[inflection: rise]` - Forces an upward pitch-shift at the end of rhetorical questions or surprising facts.
* `[inflection: drop]` - Forces a deep downward pitch-shift for definitive, impactful punchlines.

### C. Pacing & Speed Overrides
* `[speed: 0.9x]` - Slows down the delivery of an important, high-impact statement.
* `[speed: 1.1x]` - Slightly speeds up the tempo for secondary details or quick side remarks.
* `[pause: 0.3s]` - A brief comma-length pause to separate clauses within a sentence.
* `[pause: 0.5s]` - A standard sentence-length breath pause.
* `[pause: 1.0s]` - A heavy, theatrical beat; mandatory right before a sarcastic punchline.
